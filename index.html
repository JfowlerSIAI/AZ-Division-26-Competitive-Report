Methodology & Key Findings

The points formula

EventPoints = max(1, (N - Place + 1) * Strength(EventRating)); season total = cumulative sum.

N = number of fencers in the event. Place = finish; tied fencers share a place and earn equal points; last place scores 1. Event difficulty is the USA Fencing classification: within a letter a higher number is stronger, so A2 beats A1, and earlier letters beat later ones. A2 was the strongest classification held in Arizona this season, set to full weight of 1.0. Season total is cumulative; PPE is points per event.

Data

Source: FencingTime/askFRED results and the AZ Division roster. 1073 results were scored. Competitor classification: 204 on the Division roster, 60 fencing for an Arizona club but not on the roster, 33 out-of-state. Ranked competitors are Arizona Division roster members plus others fencing for an Arizona club.

Membership. Member counts, gender, and the type breakdown come from the AZ Division roster export. The roster's Member Type field is grouped as follows. Access is USA Fencing's free introductory membership tier. Competitive includes collegiate and international competitive licenses. Professional is coaches. Life includes life-installment memberships. Supporting is parent memberships.

Division statistics. Rated-fencer counts are roster-derived: each fencer is counted once per weapon in which they hold a current letter rating. Div 1 is A/B/C and Div 3 is D/E. Potential attendees are competition-eligible members, the total minus Access and Supporting; a fencer counts toward each weapon they are rated in, and members unrated in all three are split across weapons by the rated-fencer ratio.
