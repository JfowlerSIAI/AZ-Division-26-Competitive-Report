Methodology & Key Findings

The points formula

EventPoints = max(1, (N - Place + 1) * Strength(EventRating)); season total = cumulative sum. Last place always scores >=1 to encourage attendance.

N = number of fencers in the event; Place = finish (ties share a place → equal points; last place = 1, floored so attendance always counts). Event difficulty is the USA Fencing classification: within a letter a higher number is stronger (A2 beats A1) and earlier letters beat later ones. A2 was the strongest classification held in Arizona this season and is set to full weight, 1.0, with the scale dropping more steeply toward the low end. Cumulative totals reward consistent competition; PPE is shown for quality.

Membership. Member counts, gender, and the type breakdown come from the AZ Division roster export. The roster's Member Type field is grouped as follows. Non-Competitive is the Access tier, USA Fencing's free introductory membership. Competitive includes collegiate and international competitive licenses. Professional is coaches. Life includes life-installment memberships. Supporting is parent memberships.

Division statistics. Rated-fencer counts are roster-derived: each fencer is counted once per weapon in which they hold a current letter rating (Div 1 = A/B/C, Div 3 = D/E). Potential athletes = competition-eligible members (total minus Non-Competitive and Supporting); a fencer counts toward each weapon they are rated in, and members unrated in all three are split across weapons by the rated-fencer ratio.
